Provided Services

KADC Town adopt a philosophy of thorough, routine examinations, prevention, education and early intervention,
to maintained the oral health for patients.

General Dentistry

General dentistry covers a range of treatment options and procedures fundamental to protecting and maintaining a good standard of oral health.

Periodontal Treatment

The goal of periodontitis treatment is to thoroughly clean the pockets around teeth and prevent damage to surrounding bone.

Removable Dentures

Consist of replacement teeth attached to pink or gum-colored plastic or metal bases. Depending on your needs, the dentist will design a partial denture or full denture for you.

Fixed Dentures

Stays in your mouth at all times are basically denture devices that are fixed and cannot be removed by the patient is only at the dental office where this can be fix and removed.

Oral surgeries

Are any surgical procedure performed on your teeth, gums, jaws or other oral structures. This includes extractions, implants, gum grafts and other procedures.

Corrective Orthodontics

Corrective orthodontics is the treatment of a malocclusion in the permanent dentition

Preventive Orthodontics

The goal of this treatment is to keep malocclusions or ‘bad bites’ from developing.

Interceptive Orthodontics

Interceptive treatment is necessary to avoid the development of problems that could possibly lead to severe dental cases.

Restoration

Restorations are the various ways your dentist can replace or restore your missing teeth or missing parts of your tooth structure, to prevent decay that may cause you pain in the future

Root Canal Treatments

Procedure used to treat infection at the center of a tooth. Root canal treatment is not painful and can save a tooth that might otherwise have to be removed completely.

Cosmetic Dentistry

It's the medical improvement of the overall appearance of your teeth–resulting in a great smile.

Dental imagenology

A periapical x-ray is one that captures the whole tooth. It shows everything from the crown (chewing surface) to the root (below the gum line).

Sealants

Can be applied to the occlusal surfaces of teeth to penetrate anatomic surface pits and fissures and form a physical barrier on the tooth surface.
